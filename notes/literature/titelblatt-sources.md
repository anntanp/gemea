# Titelblatt / Barocke Titelform — Sources

Candidate sources for the Baroque title-page convention claim and the 18th-century shortening shift (SR-10). Not yet fully verified — see status notes per entry.

---

## Confirmed (volume / DOI known)

**Rautenberg, U. (2008).** "Die Entstehung und Entwicklung des Buchtitelblatts in der Inkunabelzeit in Deutschland, den Niederlanden und Venedig. Quantitative und qualitative Studien." *Archiv für Geschichte des Buchwesens* **63**.
- Covers title-page emergence up to ~1500 (incunabula period).
- Establishes the form and function of the early title page but stops well before the Baroque–Enlightenment transition.
- **Not directly citable for the length claim**, but useful for structural baseline.
- DOI/page: <https://www.degruyter.com/view/product/178938>

**Götz, U., Gessing, A., Neumann, M. & Woggan, A. (2017).** *Die Syntax von Titelblättern des 16. und 17. Jahrhunderts.* Lingua Historica Germanica 17. De Gruyter, Berlin/Boston. 384 pp.
- Systematic analysis of title-page syntactic structure, 16th–17th c.
- Covers Baroque elaboration as the baseline period — may support characterization of the long-title norm before the Enlightenment contraction.
- Stops at 1700; does not address the 18th-century shortening directly.
- DOI: <https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/9783110545562/html>

---

## Primary source (period evidence, not historical analysis)

**Lessing, G. E. (1767–1769).** *Hamburgische Dramaturgie.*
- Contains a programmatic statement against elaborate titles: "Der Titel soll kein Küchenzettel sein… Je weniger er von dem Inhalte verräth, desto besser ist er."
- Useful as evidence of the normative shift in the Enlightenment, not quantitative data.
- Digitized: <https://digi.ub.uni-heidelberg.de/diglit/lessing1786bd1/0034>
