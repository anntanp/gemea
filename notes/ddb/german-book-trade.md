## 1. Gutenberg and the Incunabula Era (1450–1500)

- **c. 1450**: Johannes Gutenberg develops movable metal type in Mainz; prints the 42-line Bible (~1455). (Eisenstein, *The Printing Press as an Agent of Change*, 1980, vol. 1, pp. 43–58; [WorldCat](https://www.worldcat.org/title/printing-press-as-an-agent-of-change/oclc/4415189))
- By 1500: ~250 towns across Europe have presses; ~27,000 editions printed (incunabula). (Incunabula Short Title Catalogue, British Library: [istc.bl.uk](https://www.bl.uk/catalogues/istc/); Febvre & Martin, *L'Apparition du livre*, 1958, Eng. trans. *The Coming of the Book*, 1976, p. 186)
- Mainz, Strasbourg, Cologne, and Nuremberg emerge as early printing centers. (Füssel, *Gutenberg and the Impact of Printing*, 2005, pp. 90–110; [publisher page](https://www.routledge.com/Gutenberg-and-the-Impact-of-Printing/Fssel/p/book/9780754640318))
- Frankfurt already functions as a trade hub. (Wittmann, *Geschichte des deutschen Buchhandels*, 3rd ed. 2011, p. 26; [C.H. Beck](https://www.chbeck.de/wittmann-geschichte-deutschen-buchhandels/product/11577))

## 2. Reformation and Religious Print War (1517–1555)

- **1517**: Luther's 95 Theses — mass-produced pamphlets (*Flugschriften*) become the first print media campaign. (Edwards, *Printing, Propaganda, and Martin Luther*, 1994, pp. 15–35; [UC Press](https://www.ucpress.edu/book/9780520088399/printing-propaganda-and-martin-luther))
- Luther's German Bible (1534) standardizes written German and drives print demand. (Flood, "The Book in Reformation Germany," in *The Reformation and the Book*, ed. Gilmont, 1998, pp. 21–103; [Ashgate](https://www.routledge.com/The-Reformation-and-the-Book/Gilmont/p/book/9780754601289))
- **1555**: Peace of Augsburg. (Schilling, *Die neue Zeit*, 1999; for primary text see [Avalon Project, Yale](https://avalon.law.yale.edu/))

## 3. Frankfurt Book Fair as Trade Institution (16th–17th c.)

- Frankfurt Book Fair *Messkatalog* begins **1564**; runs until 1860. Digitized by the Herzog August Bibliothek Wolfenbüttel: [diglib.hab.de](https://diglib.hab.de/?link=079) (search "Messkatalog")
- Booksellers as publishers (*Verleger*): Wittmann, *Geschichte des deutschen Buchhandels*, pp. 39–55.
- Latin/vernacular shift: Flood, "The Frankfurt Book Fair," in *Fairs, Markets and the Itinerant Book Trade*, ed. Walsby & Constantinidou, 2013, pp. 103–128.

## 4. Thirty Years' War and Aftermath (1618–1680)

- War disrupts trade routes, paper supply, and demand. (Wittmann, pp. 70–82)
- Leipzig's rise as the new center: Lehmstedt, *Buchstadt Leipzig*, 2008, pp. 12–30; [Lehmstedt Verlag](https://www.lehmstedt.de/buchstadt-leipzig.html)
- Saxon Protestant patronage and the Leipzig university press tradition: cf. Paisey, "Buchdruck, Buchhandel und Universität," *Wolfenbütteler Notizen zur Buchgeschichte* 11 (1986).

## 5. Leipzig Ascendancy and Commission Trade (1700–1800)

- Leipzig Book Fair overtakes Frankfurt by ~1700. (Wittmann, pp. 90–105; Goldfriedrich, *Geschichte des deutschen Buchhandels*, vol. 2, 1908, pp. 1–50 — digitized at [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/geschichtedesdeu02gold))
- Commission system (*Kommissionsbuchhandel*): Jäger, "Der Kommissionshandel des deutschen Buchhandels," in *Handbuch des deutschen Buchhandels*, vol. 2, 2003.
- **1765**: Friedrich Nicolai's *Allgemeine deutsche Bibliothek*. (Möller, *Aufklärung in Preußen*, 1974; full run digitized at [Göttinger Digitalisierungszentrum](https://gdz.sub.uni-goettingen.de/id/PPN362706905))
- Enlightenment booksellers: Wittmann, pp. 117–135.

## 6. Booksellers' Association and Copyright (1825–1870)

- **1825**: *Börsenverein des Deutschen Buchhandels* founded. Official history: Wittmann; primary: *Börsenblatt für den deutschen Buchhandel* (1834–present), digitized 1834–1945 at [Zeitschriftendatenbank / MDZ München](https://www.digitale-sammlungen.de/de/view/bsb00069929): [direct link](https://www.digitale-sammlungen.de/de/view/bsb00069929)
- **1835** Prussian copyright extended to Zollverein: Kawohl, "Commentary on the Prussian Act," in *Primary Sources on Copyright (1450–1900)*, ed. Deazley, Kretschmer & Bently, 2008: [copyrighthistory.org](http://www.copyrighthistory.org/cam/tools/request/showRecord.php?id=commentary_d_1837)
- Fixed retail price development: Spindler, *Die Buchpreisbindung*, 2007.
- Reclam Universal-Bibliothek (**1867**): [Reclam official history](https://www.reclam.de/unternehmen/history)

## 7. German Unification and Industrial Book Production (1871–1914)

- **1871** Reich unification, unified copyright jurisdiction. (Wittmann, pp. 205–225)
- **Copyright law 1901** (extended 1907), Berne Convention: WIPO historical records, [wipo.int/treaties/en/ip/berne/](https://www.wipo.int/treaties/en/ip/berne/)
- *Deutsches Buchgewerbehaus* (1900) and *Deutsche Bücherei* (1912), Leipzig: Deutsche Nationalbibliothek history, [dnb.de/EN/Kulturell/Geschichte](https://www.dnb.de/EN/Kulturell/Geschichte/geschichte_node.html)
- Colportage and railway bookstalls: Schenda, *Volk ohne Buch*, 1970.
- Major publishing houses: Fischer, *Der S. Fischer Verlag* (catalog histories); Brockhaus history at [brockhaus.de/ueber-uns](https://www.brockhaus.de/ueber-uns/)

## 8. Weimar Republic and Political Publishing (1919–1933)

- Competing political publishers: Kaes, Jay & Dimendberg, eds., *The Weimar Republic Sourcebook*, 1994, pp. 634–668; [UC Press](https://www.ucpress.edu/book/9780520067752/the-weimar-republic-sourcebook)
- **1923 hyperinflation** and recovery: Wittmann, pp. 290–310.
- Book clubs (*Buchgemeinschaften*): Langenbucher, *Der aktuelle Unterhaltungsroman*, 1964.
- Ullstein Verlag: Schwerin von Krosigk, *Die Ullsteins*, 2001.

## 9. Nazi Book Trade (1933–1945)

- **May 10, 1933** book burnings: documentation at Yad Vashem, [yadvashem.org](https://www.yadvashem.org/odot_pdf/Microsoft%20Word%20-%203875.pdf); USHMM, [encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/book-burning](https://encyclopedia.ushmm.org/content/en/article/book-burning)
- *Reichsschrifttumskammer*: Barbian, *Literaturpolitik im NS-Staat*, 2010 (Eng. trans. *The Politics of Literature in Nazi Germany*, 2013); [Bloomsbury](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/politics-of-literature-in-nazi-germany-9781441140807/)
- Exile publishers: Sternfeld & Tiedemann, *Deutsche Exil-Literatur 1933–1945*, 1970; cf. [Exilarchiv, DNB](https://www.dnb.de/DE/Kulturell/Exilarchiv/exilarchiv_node.html)
- Leipzig bombing: Lehmstedt, *Buchstadt Leipzig*, pp. 180–200.

## 10. Division and Reunification (1945–1990)

- *Börsenverein* reconstituted in Frankfurt **1948**; Fair relaunched **1949**: Frankfurter Buchmesse official history, [buchmesse.de/en/about-us/history](https://www.buchmesse.de/en/about-us/history)
- **Peace Prize of the German Book Trade** (1950–): [boersenverein.de/en/frankfurt-book-fair/peace-prize/](https://www.boersenverein.de/en/frankfurt-book-fair/peace-prize/)
- GDR publishing (*Volkseigene Verlage*), HV Verlage censorship: Murray, *The Unfinished Revolution: Making Sense of the Communist Past in Central-Eastern Europe*, 1997; cf. Barck, Langermann & Lokatis, *"Jedes Buch ein Abenteuer"*, 1997.
- **1952** split of *Börsenverein*: Wittmann, pp. 370–390.
- **1990** reunification of branches: *Börsenblatt* 57 (1990), no. 68, p. 2934.
